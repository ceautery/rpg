Session Log — Stone Cellar

Ruleset: SRD 5.2 (srd-2024, CC-BY 4.0)

Party: Aldric Sootmantle (agent · wizard · level 1)

Session start: 2026-06-20

Scene — Stone Cellar

The last grey light of dusk has long since failed to reach down here. Aldric Sootmantle descends a short flight of crumbling stone steps — more a collapsed embankment than proper stairs — and finds himself in a low-ceilinged cellar that smells of mildew, rat musk, and something sharper: the acrid bite of tallow smoke.

The chamber is small, maybe twenty feet across at its widest, narrowing toward a rubble-choked back wall. The ceiling hovers barely six feet overhead, so the room feels compressed, almost suffocating. Water has seeped through the mortared joints in the walls and left long rust-brown streaks. Broken clay amphorae lie in the corners, their contents long since dried to a dark crust. A single stub of tallow candle gutters atop an upturned crate near the far wall, throwing orange light that barely reaches the middle of the room.

Crouched behind that crate, half-lit by the candle, is a goblin. It is small even by goblin standards, its greenish skin mottled with old scars, ears notched and ragged. It is clutching something — a leather-bound book, roughly the size that might once have lived in a scholar's satchel. Your satchel. Your grimoire.

The goblin has not yet seen Aldric. It is hunched over the book with its back angled toward the entrance, tracing a clawed fingertip along the cover as if reading the brass clasp by touch alone.

Goblin (muttering): "Shiny lock, shiny lock... must be worth something, yes. Fence it to Dobb? Or keep? Keep is better. Keep is always better."

Initiative: Skrix 15 · Aldric 14 (Skrix acts first)

Turn 1 — Stone Cellar

Actions:

  • Skrix (monster): unaware of Aldric — spends turn working the grimoire's brass clasp, muttering "...stubborn thing. What wizard puts a lock on a book?" (no mechanic; no roll)
  • Aldric (agent · wizard): raises arcane focus from the darkness at B1 and casts Magic Missile (1st-level slot) at the goblin, three darts of force — "Put. The book. Down."

Rolls:

· r2 · Magic Missile dart 1 · 1d4+1 = 5 force · auto-hit (no attack roll)
· r3 · Magic Missile dart 2 · 1d4+1 = 5 force · auto-hit
· r4 · Magic Missile dart 3 · 1d4+1 = 3 force · auto-hit
· Total: 13 force damage → Skrix 6 HP → 0 HP (overkill 7)

Outcome:

Aldric does not step into the light. He does not need to.

From the darkness at the room's north end, he extends two fingers and speaks the words — barely a whisper, lips barely parting, the syllables more felt than heard. Three motes of cold blue-white light bloom at his fingertips and streak across the cellar in the same heartbeat.

Skrix never turns around.

The first bolt takes him between the shoulder blades. The second catches the side of his neck. The third punches into the ear he has pressed close to the grimoire's cover, as if listening for something inside it. There is no cry, no scramble, no reaching for a weapon. The force is not fire — it makes no sound at all except a low, resonant thud, like a knuckle rapping on a hollow door, three times in rapid succession.

The goblin folds.

His knees give first, then his grip. The grimoire slides from his fingers and drops onto the crate with a flat slap, brass clasp still locked, entirely undamaged. Skrix tips sideways off his crouch and slumps onto the packed-earth floor behind the crate. The tallow candle rocks in the small draft of his falling but does not go out.

Silence reclaims the cellar. The candle stub burns on, indifferent. The grimoire sits atop the crate in the guttering orange light, waiting.

Aldric said: "Put. The book. Down."

It is fair to say that Skrix complied.

Scene cleared. Aldric recovers his mentor's grimoire. XP awarded: 50.

Scene — Guard Post Chamber

Stone Bauer ducks through the low entry and takes in the cellar in a glance: dead goblin, spent candle, the smell of something charred. He nods once — the way soldiers do when words would only waste time.

At the south end of the cellar, half-concealed behind a collapsed shelf of rotted planks, is a low arch in the stone wall. A short passage drops three steps beyond it. The smell drifting back is earthier: wet stone, old iron, torch smoke that has been burning longer than Skrix's candle was.

As the two of them approach, a voice comes from the far room — flat, waiting.

Goblin (from the darkness beyond the arch): "Skrix. You drop something again, or you dead?"

A pause. The silence answers for Skrix. The sound of a bowstring being drawn back — slow, deliberate — carries clearly through the stone arch.

This one already knows.

The passage opens into a flagstone chamber, bright with torchlight. In the southeast corner, crouched behind an iron-banded barrel with a shortbow already drawn and aimed at chest height — aimed at whoever comes through the arch first — is a goblin. It watches the opening without blinking.

Initiative: Stone 22 · Vreck 20 · Aldric 14 (Stone acts first)

Turn 2, Round 1 — Guard Post Chamber

Actions:

  • Stone (human · fighter): charges through arch, throws handaxe at Vreck
  • Vreck (monster): readied shortbow fires at Stone as he enters (reaction)
  • Aldric (agent · wizard): moves to rubble at B2, casts Fire Bolt at Vreck

Rolls:

· r1 · Vreck readied shortbow vs Stone · 1d20+4 = 14 vs AC 19 → MISS
· r2 · Stone handaxe throw vs Vreck · 1d20+5 = 19 vs AC 20 → MISS (missed by 1 — barrel)
· r3 · Vreck Hide (Stealth) · 1d20+6 = 7 vs Passive Perception 10 → FAIL (natural 1)
· r4 · Aldric Fire Bolt vs Vreck · 1d20+5 = 16 vs AC 20 → MISS

Outcome:

Stone does not wait. The moment his boots clear the arch he is already moving — three long strides across the flagstones, closing the distance in a straight line toward the barrel in the southeast corner. His hand comes off his belt mid-run and the handaxe is spinning before Vreck has time to track him.

Vreck's arrow comes first.

The shortbow snaps and the shaft hisses across the room at chest height, aimed at whoever dared charge through that arch. It catches nothing. Stone is already leaning into the run, angle wrong, the arrow clipping past his left shoulder with a whisper of displaced air and clattering against the far wall. Fourteen on the roll. Nineteen AC to beat. One step either way and it would have been a different story.

The handaxe arrives a heartbeat later and rings off the iron-banded stave of the barrel with a hard, bright clang — a sound that fills the whole room — and spins away to the floor, harmless. The barrel did not so much as rock. Nineteen total on the throw. Twenty to beat. The margin of a single pip.

Vreck pulls his head down below the barrel's rim and tries to vanish into the corner. He is not subtle about it. The torch on the west wall lights the room without mercy and the goblin is plainly visible — one notched ear poking above the barrel's lip, one yellow eye tracking Stone from across the room. Both men can see him perfectly.

From the rubble pile, Aldric extends two fingers and speaks a single syllable. A jet of orange-red fire streaks toward the barrel, toward the goblin — and misses clean. The flame licks the stone wall above the barrel and leaves a dark char-mark, nothing more.

The room goes quiet except for the torch guttering on the west wall. Vreck is uninjured. The barrel has stopped everything.

Round 2. Stone acts first.

Turn 2, Round 2 — Guard Post Chamber

Actions:

  • Stone (human · fighter): closes to C3 (adjacent), attacks Vreck with longsword
  • Vreck (monster): scimitar counter-attack vs Stone (no OA — Stone moved in)
  • Aldric (agent · wizard): stays at B2, fires 3 Magic Missile darts at Vreck (last spell slot)

Rolls:

· r1 · Stone longsword vs Vreck · 1d20+5 = 8 vs AC 15 → MISS (rolled a 3)
· r4 · Vreck scimitar vs Stone · 1d20+4 = 13 vs AC 19 → MISS
· r5 · Magic Missile dart 1 · 1d4+1 = 4 force · auto-hit
· r6 · Magic Missile dart 2 · 1d4+1 = 4 force · auto-hit
· r7 · Magic Missile dart 3 · 1d4+1 = 2 force · auto-hit
· Total Magic Missile: 10 force damage → Vreck 6 HP → 0 HP

Outcome:

Round 2.

Stone closes the gap to the barrel with two heavy strides and brings his longsword around in a broad arc — the kind of swing a fighter makes when he wants a thing finished. The blade catches nothing. It passes over the lip of the barrel by a hand's width, the edge skipping off empty air, and Stone stumbles half a step, weight committed to a blow that never landed. He recovers his footing, sets his jaw, and says nothing. He is very used to saying nothing.

Vreck's yellow eye appears above the barrel rim. The goblin has drawn his scimitar — no room for the shortbow at this range — and he lunges out from the cover in a quick, vicious slash aimed at the gap between Stone's pauldron and his collar. The scimitar finds the gap. The edge does not. It glances off the shoulder plate with a sound like a coin dropped on flagstone, and Vreck's arm rebounds from the impact hard enough to rattle his teeth. Nineteen armour. The goblin might as well have swung at a door.

For a stretched, almost comic moment, both of them stand there — Stone looming, sword out, having missed clean; Vreck on the wrong side of his own swing, wrist stinging, one eye now tracking something over Stone's shoulder.

The three motes of blue-white light arrive before Vreck can finish the thought.

Aldric does not move from the rubble pile. He simply raises two fingers, the same unhurried gesture as before, and speaks the words. The darts materialise already at their target, punching into Vreck's chest and left shoulder and the meat of his sword arm with three low resonant thuds. Ten force damage. Six hit points available to receive it.

The scimitar drops first. Then Vreck.

The goblin folds against the barrel and slides down it, the iron banding leaving a slow drag-mark on his vest. He does not get up.

Stone looks at the crumpled goblin. He looks at his sword. He looks across the room at Aldric.

Stone: "Good hit."

Scene cleared. XP awarded: 50 (Vreck). Party totals: 100 XP.

Aldric: 0/2 spell slots remaining. Stone: 4/4 HP. Aldric: 7/7 HP.

Rest — Long Rest · Undercroft

The undercroft is quiet now. Two dead goblins, a recovered grimoire, a scorched wall. Stone drags the barrel across the arch opening and sits with his back against it while Aldric pages through the grimoire by the light of the remaining torch stub, checking for damage that isn't there. They trade the watch in silence. By the time pale light filters down through the cracks above, both of them have slept.

Resources restored (long rest):

  • Aldric: spell slots 2/2 · HP 7/7 · Hit Dice 1/1
  • Stone: HP 4/4 · Second Wind 1/1 · Hit Dice 1/1

Scene — Goblin Treasury

The south door from the guard post opens on a short, descending passage — six feet of rough-cut stone that drops two steps before leveling into the next chamber. The smell changes immediately: copper and tallow and something sweeter beneath both, the particular musk of hoarded things kept in the dark too long.

The treasury is a low, wide room, roughly thirty feet across and twenty deep, with a ceiling that slopes down toward the far wall until a tall man would have to duck. Flagstones cover the floor in uneven slabs, some slightly raised at their edges, others sunken, the mortar between them eroded to black gaps. Two iron cressets on the east and west walls hold actual torches, not stubs: this room is maintained. Someone lives here.

At the far end, against the south wall, sits a squat iron chest, banded in black iron and fitted with a heavy padlock. Around it, scattered loose — the way a crow's nest looks — are bones, bent copper coins, a cracked clay jug, and scraps of cloth. The wealth of the undercroft, such as it is.

And in front of the chest, standing deliberately — because he wants to be seen — is Gruk.

Gruk: "I hear Skrix fall. I hear Vreck fall. Very quiet now, very quick. You are not rats."

Gruk: "I am Gruk. I took this hole from the last ones who had it. Whatever you want from it — you take it from me."

Initiative: Stone 14 · Aldric 11 · Gruk 5 (Stone acts first)

Turn 3, Round 1 — Goblin Treasury

Actions:

  • Stone (human · fighter): charges C3 → E3 → F4, attacks Gruk with longsword
  • Aldric (agent · wizard): stays at B2, casts Magic Missile (1st-level slot) at Gruk — 3 darts
  • Gruk (monster): flee triggered (HP ≤ 6 after PC actions) — Nimble Escape + crawl hole

Rolls:

· r1 · Flagstone trap (E3) crossbow bolt vs Stone · 1d20+4 = 16 vs AC 19 → MISS
· r2 · Stone longsword vs Gruk · 1d20+5 = 6 vs AC 17 → MISS (natural 1)
· r3 · Magic Missile dart 1 · 1d4+1 = 2 force · auto-hit
· r4 · Magic Missile dart 2 · 1d4+1 = 4 force · auto-hit
· r5 · Magic Missile dart 3 · 1d4+1 = 5 force · auto-hit
· Total Magic Missile: 11 force → Gruk 12 HP → 1 HP (flee threshold reached — Gruk runs)

Outcome:

Stone moves before Gruk finishes his last thought.

He comes hard and fast — C3 to D3 in two strides, angling south toward the chest, momentum building. His boot heel comes down on the flagstone at E3 and the pressure plate does exactly what it was built to do: a bolt snaps out of a slot in the east wall at shin height, iron-tipped, fast. It misses. Stone's line of travel has carried him past the release point by the time the mechanism fires, the bolt crossing a half-step behind his trailing leg and burying itself in the gap between two flagstones with a flat, hard thunk. He does not even flinch. He may not have noticed.

He arrives at F4 with his longsword already clearing the scabbard and puts everything into the swing — a committed, close-range arc aimed at Gruk's torso. The blade catches nothing. His wrist torques wrong at the last moment, the steel overshooting, and Stone stumbles forward half a step into empty space, sword arm extended past the point of no return. He recovers his footing by instinct alone, turns, and finds Gruk still standing in front of the chest, staring at him.

Aldric, at B2, does not wait to see how Stone will recover.

Three motes of cold blue-white light bloom from his outstretched fingers. The first dart crosses the room in a flat line and strikes Gruk in the chest, just below the collarbone — a resonant thud, force with no fire, no arc, no warning. The second catches him in the right shoulder before he can begin to turn. The third punches into his side between the second and third rib, and for a moment Gruk just stands there, absorbing eleven points of force damage with the expression of something that cannot quite believe the math.

He does not try to fight through it.

Gruk moves — sideways and back, one arm shooting out and shoving the iron chest aside with surprising strength for a creature at one hit point. It scrapes a full foot across the flagstones and reveals what it had been sitting against: a low opening in the southeast wall, maybe two feet by two feet, the kind of gap that a goblin fits through and a human does not. He does not say anything. He does not look back. He folds himself into the crawl hole and is gone, the darkness of the passage swallowing him in three seconds flat.

The scrape of iron on stone fades. The torches in their cressets burn on, indifferent.

Stone stands at the south end of the room, longsword extended, in front of a displaced iron chest and an empty square of black nothing in the southeast wall.

Stone: "Missed a goblin with my whole body. And it had one hit point." He sheathes the longsword. "There's a chest. Gruk took the key with him."

Aldric: "He was at one HP. He made the correct decision." He crouches near E3 and examines the slot in the east wall. "Pressure plate here — mechanism is spent. Mind the flagstones on the way back regardless. I would want to be certain there is only one." He looks at the chest. "Lock or force. Your preference."

Scene cleared. Gruk fled (1/12 HP) via crawl hole. XP awarded: 100 (Gruk, partial — fled). Party totals: 200 XP.

Trap at E3 revealed (spent). Crawl hole at F4 revealed. Iron chest at F4 — padlocked, key fled with Gruk.

Aldric: 1/2 spell slots remaining. Stone: 4/4 HP. Aldric: 7/7 HP.

Loot — Iron Chest

Stone plants his boot against the hasp and shoves. 18 on Athletics. The old iron gives with a sound like a muffled gunshot — the padlock swings free, hasp bent, shackle sheared from the housing. He steps back.

Aldric raises two fingers. The spectral hand drifts forward across thirty feet of flagstone and lifts the lid from a safe distance. Nothing fires. The lid rises on corroded hinges and stops.

Inside: a scatter of loose silver coins — ten, mixed with a single heavier gold piece that has settled to the bottom — and a small glass vial on a leather cord, stoppered with dark wax, the paper label faded past reading. And beneath those, tucked into a gap between the chest wall and the false bottom, a folded square of paper.

Aldric crosses the room and picks up the vial first, holding it to the torchlight. The liquid inside is faintly pink, viscous. He sets it down and takes the folded note. He reads it. He reads it again.

He does not say anything for a moment.

Aldric: "This is my mentor's handwriting."

The note reads, in a cramped, familiar hand:

"If you have found this, then I was wrong about Gruk. He was not the one who hired them. Look north — the Dobb name came up twice in Crestfall market. That is not coincidence."

Loot divided:

  • 10 sp split evenly (5 sp each — Stone and Aldric)
  • 1 gp (Aldric, toward future supplies)
  • Potion of Healing, 2d4+2 HP (Aldric — he is the squishier target)
  • Folded note in mentor's hand (Aldric)

They leave the goblin to his crawl hole.

Stone goes first, back through the guard post, back through the cellar, up the crumbling stone steps into morning air that smells of wet grass and woodsmoke and nothing at all like mildew. He takes a long breath. Aldric follows with the grimoire under one arm and the vial on its cord tucked inside his coat, the folded note in his hand, reading it a third time.

The mill sits quiet above them. The road north is dry.

Stone: "You think your mentor's still alive?"

Aldric: "I think someone went to considerable trouble to make sure I couldn't answer that question." He folds the note and pockets it. "Crestfall is north. Dobb is a name. That is more than I had yesterday."

They walk.

Session end. Party: Aldric Sootmantle · Stone Bauer. Total XP: 200. Quest complete: Clear the Undercroft. Open hook: The Dobb Name (Crestfall, north).
